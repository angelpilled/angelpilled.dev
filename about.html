Short Welcome

Hello boyos! Welcome to angelpilled's about page! I want to share a bit of an info about myself, hopefully you'll find this reading interesting.

The Name "angelpilled"

My name, "angelpilled" means more than just a string of characters to me. It resonates with a connection of otherworldly beings, entities that are beyond us humans. I picked this name in the beginning on 2022, but made my presence online in May 2023 with this alias. This is my attempt to express that mythical feeling.

My Website

This website isn't only a place for me to show you my hobbies, it's my way of expressing any feelings or thoughts that I have.

I started hosting this website circa May, 2023, when I wanted to try something new. I've never hosted a website before, but have developed personal pages prior for a few years.

Needless to say, I was amazed at what I could do. I've always made shitty ass portfolio websites that even I didn't like, but I got to a point where I gathered a lot of inspiration from neocities websites.

Because I had a lot of free time on my hand, I could easily spend 7-8 hours a day just to research web development, and add something new to my website each day. Personally, I'm proud of myself for making this website. It's not perfect, but I like the look of it.

This went on for a few weeks, maybe 2. Then I stopped altogether because le lack of motivation. I haven't touched any part of my website for 3 months.

Now, after a long summer in 2023, I can safely say, on August 27th, my web development journey starts again.

Maybe the change I needed was a new environment. Not in a real-life sense, but software-wise. I completely switched to Arch Linux in the beginning of 2023's summer, after having to deal with Windows' bullshit for months. I finally don't have to dual boot and I don't have to worry about Windows updates erasing my BIOS linux boot entries.

Experiences with Linux

There's probably a blog on this topic by the time you're reading this, but I'll just write a really short wall of text about this subject.

I got into the realms of Linux before the summer of 2022. I ran multiple distros on virtual machines because I couldn't risk running any distro natively.

I started with Ubuntu, had a really negative experience, then tried out a few more distros and now I'm here, running an Arch Linux. My distro hopping finally stopped.

I might have been 13 years old when I first tried out Linux though. At that time I had a friend who ran Ubuntu on her machine. Weird, I though. She said her father have always used Linux and that's what he installed on her PC.

Looking back on it now, I wish I would have made an effort trying to understand Linux when I was that young. At that time it seemed really grim, and I wasn't wrong; Ubuntu really isn't my cup of tea (and I wrote this in a light-hearted manner).

I am now running Arch Linux on all my machines, and it's really comfy now. Having a flexible bloatless and free system is all that matters to me. Installing packages haven't been easier on any other distro that I've tried.

That's all...
